Instructions (click to collapse / expand)

In this task, you will use your knowledge about language (and common sense) to determine if the second sentence is true given that the first sentence is true.

Task Definition

  • Carefully read the two given sentences
  • The two sentences are identical apart from a verb that was dropped
  • Given the first sentence is true, is the second sentence always true?
    e.g. given that the following sentence is true:
    "She started the book she bought yesterday", also: "She started reading the book she bought yesterday" is true (and the answer is Yes).
  • It won't always be the case, as the sentence meaning can change sometimes:
    Given that the following is true "They started the restaurant", the sentence: "They started reaching the restaurant" isn't true.
    As in the first sentence some entities established or built a restaurant, whereas in the second sentence,
    some entities are arriving to some restaurant. Therefore, the answer is No
    In case the answer is No, you will have to add a complement verb(s) to the first sentence that explains this answer. In this case, the verb "created" is a good candidate. See the examples for more details.
  • In the cases where the answer to the question is No, a followup question will be asked
    You will be shown two expressions, that comes from the original sentence. For example, in the case of the previous example "started the restaurant" and "started reaching the restaurant". Can the first expression have the same meaning of the second one?
    You will have to choose between Yes and No. In the case of this example, the answer would be No, as it has a completely different meaning.
  • In other cases, the meaning of the second phrase can still be inferred from the first phrase. For example. given the following sentence is true" "He continued the meal", "He continued preparing the meal" is not always true. Therefore, the answer to the first quesiton is No. Then, the answer to the second question, would be Yes, as "continued the meal" can inferred as "continued preparing the meal".
    A good substitute for this verb is "eating".
  • Notice that substitute verbs would be presented right after the question to help think about alternative scenarios where the answer is not Yes
  • Notice that the sentences might contain space between words and punctuation marks. This is OK, treat them usually.
  • When providing a clarifying verb (in the cases of "Sometimes" and "No"), the verb which is given in the second sentence, or another verb with a similar meaning, do not count as clarifying verbs.
Make sure to read all the examples and their explanations to get a notion of the different cases
